(**Draft**) South Platte and Metro Basin Water Entities (**Draft**)

This story highlights the various types of water entities in the South Platte and Metro basins, such as municipalities, municipal water providers, agriculture, environmental, recreational and industrial entities.

The purpose of this story is to provide general context for the number, location and extent of such entities in the basin.

Municipal Water Use and Efficiency

Cities in the South Platte and Metro basins are national leaders in water conservation and reuse (BIP, p.1-17). Municipal conservation programs have already been heavily implemented in the larger cities. This map shows water providers that have water efficiency plans.

Map shows the municipalities that have plans in a different color. Maybe size the municipalities by current population to see that the largest cities are the ones that have efficiency plans. Can click on links to plan websites in the mouse-over.

Additional reuse of water supply components including nontributary groundwater, the consumptive use component of changed native water rights and transbasin imports is possible but may affect downstream water availability, water management flexibility and interstate water compact compliance (BIP, p.1-17).
